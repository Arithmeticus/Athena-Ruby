fully Unicode compliant (optimal for data interchange, but may not look right in your browser; ideal for getting Unicode data, without regard to special glyphs)

using Private Use Area (not optimal for data interchange, but should look correct; ideal for use in most word processors)

Tested successfully in Chrome, Firefox, Opera, Safari for Mac. Looks best in Chrome, Firefox. When you paste text in your other application, make sure that you have Athena Ruby installed, that you paste text only, and that you paste it in a section styled with Athena Ruby.

Beware:

  • Athena glyphs used for both Latin and Greek (an N will convert to a Greek nu, but you may have meant a Latin N; a Å could signify A or D)
  • Macrons (you may need to supply extra macrons [U+0305] in the converted text)
